# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Individual Christians across traditions — Orthodox, Roman Catholic, Anglican, Protestant, and Evangelical — building a daily prayer habit. The app must feel like home to all of them, so its language stays ecumenical: it avoids tradition-specific jargon (e.g. "collect," "canticle," "examen") and leans, where a distinctive phrase is genuinely needed, on early-church wording common to the whole Church. They open the app in private moments — early morning, late evening, a quiet break — often alone and sometimes in low light. They are not power users hunting features; they want to show up, pray, and leave settled. The job to be done: make space for daily prayer and gently sustain the practice over time, through guided prayers, reminders, and a private record of their walk.

## Product Purpose

A personal prayer companion that helps a believer return to prayer day after day. It holds guided prayers, a private prayer list/journal, and unobtrusive reminders, and quietly tracks faithfulness over time without turning devotion into a game. Success is not engagement metrics; it is a person who prayed today, and who feels the app honored that rather than gamified it.

## Core Mechanic: The Adaptive Rule of Prayer

The heart of the app is an adaptive **rule of prayer** that meets a person exactly where they are and grows only on demonstrated faithfulness. It never pushes past what they can actually sustain.

- **Assessment first.** A short onboarding survey finds the starting point (chiefly: do you currently pray morning and evening prayers, and how established is the routine?) and places the user on the lowest rung they can keep.
- **A ladder of rungs.** Each rung defines a concrete daily goal — a morning practice and, on later rungs, an evening one — with a target time and structure. The arc: non-prayer → a very short morning prayer → an established morning prayer → adding a short evening prayer → deepening the morning toward 10–15 minutes of devotional time with scripture → deepening the evening. Morning is established before evening is added; depth and scripture come only after consistency.
- **Daily check-in is a simple yes/no:** *Did you keep your goal today?* That single signal drives the engine.
- **Adapt down on misses.** When the user is not keeping the goal, the rule breaks into smaller, easier chunks until compliance is high again. Simplifying is a normal, gentle response, never a failure.
- **Advance on faithfulness, by invitation.** When the user sustains a rung (default target ~5 of 7 days; exact thresholds configurable and to be tuned), the app *offers* the next rung and the user opts in. Advancement is never automatic or pressured.
- **The thresholds and rung breakdown are configurable** and will be refined; the engine is built to make tuning them easy.

This mechanic is the literal embodiment of the "faithfulness, gently kept" principle: the record encourages return and earns depth, but absence is met with a smaller goal, not shame.

## Brand Personality

Grounded, timeless, reverent. Three words: rooted, quiet, substantial. The voice is that of a trustworthy companion steeped in tradition — scriptural without being preachy, encouraging without being chirpy. It speaks plainly and warmly, never with hype or guilt. Typography and material carry weight; the interface feels like a well-bound book, not a notification feed.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboard.** No metric cards, charts, KPI tiles, or startup-blue. This is not a productivity tool dressed in faith.
- **Gamified / childish.** No confetti, loud badges, cartoon mascots, or aggressive streak pressure that shames a missed day.
- **Preachy / heavy-handed.** No cluttered scripture walls, guilt-driven nudges, or dated church-bulletin aesthetics.
- **Trendy / flashy.** No neon gradients, decorative glassmorphism, or attention-grabbing motion. Restraint over spectacle.

## Design Principles

- **Reverence over engagement.** Every choice serves a calm, sacred experience, not time-on-app. When a feature would pressure or distract, cut it.
- **Substantial, not decorative.** Weight comes from typography, space, and material honesty — like a printed prayer book — never from ornament or effects.
- **Faithfulness, gently kept.** Track continuity to encourage return, but never punish absence. The record is a companion, not a scoreboard.
- **Plain and trustworthy.** Speak directly and warmly. Scripture and prompts are offered, never imposed.
- **At home in the dark.** The night-prayer context is a first-class case, not an afterthought theme.
- **Ecumenical by design.** One app for the whole Church. Language and structure stay common to all traditions; no denominational jargon. When a distinctive phrase is warranted, reach for early-church wording, sparingly.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA as a floor, AAA for body text where feasible. A true, well-tuned dark theme is essential — this is the primary evening/night-prayer surface, not a toggle bolted on. Maintain strong contrast in both themes, comfortable type sizes for older eyes, generous touch targets, and full `prefers-reduced-motion` support. Low-light legibility (warm, non-glaring luminance at night) is an explicit design constraint.
